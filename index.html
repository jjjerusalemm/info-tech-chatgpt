Technology

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a conversational AI system created by OpenAI. It is built on a large language model(LLM) that was trained on huge amounts of text from books, articles, and websites. The model predicts the next word in a sentence, one word at a time, which allows it to respond to questions, summaries, and prompts in a natural way.

What ChatGPT can do

  • Answer questions in real time
  • Summarize articles, documents, or long tests
  • Assist with writing, brainstorming, and content creation
  • support coding, debugging, and technical problem-solving

It does not “think” like a human. Instead, it uses patterns in data to generate likely responses. which is powerful, but sometimes inaccurate.

Why this matters

ChatGPT represents a major shift in how humans interact with technology. Moving from clicking buttons to having conversational, intelligent tools.

How it works

How ChatGPT works

ChatGPT operates by utilizing a large language model trained on vast amounts of text. It identifies patterns in language and predicts the most likely next word in a sentence. This process is repeated at super high speed, allowing this tool to generate full answers, explanations, stories, or code.

Key steps in how it works:

  • Input: The user types a question or message.
  • Processing: The model analyzes the text using learned patterns.
  • Prediction: It chooses the most likely next words based on probability.
  • Output: It produces a response that appears conversational and humanlike.

NOTE: ChatGPT does not understand feelings or think independently; it relies on patterns from data, not human reasoning.

Application of ChatGPT: Education Case Study

A Case Study Investigating the Utilizations of ChatGPT in Online Discussions(2023)

What happened: Researchers examined how students used ChatGPT during online course discussions in a college-level class. The study analyzed

  • How often students consulted ChatGPT
  • what kinds of responses Chatgpt generated
  • Whether it improved the quality and depth of student discussion
  • Student feedback using ChatGPT as a learning support tool

    • Students were instructed to use ChatGPT as a supplement (not replacement) for thier own thinking, and the researchers compared discussion quality before and after ChatGPT was introduced

    Why it matters: This case shows real real-life example of how AI tools can support real academic learning rather than replacing it. It reveals:

  • Students used ChatGPT to clarify ideas, brainstorm explanations, and improve writing clarity.
  • When used responsibly, ChatGPT enhanced participation in online discussions
  • AI can help students who struggle with writing or confidence, making education more equitable.
  • It raises important ethical questions about academic integrity, prompting schools to create guidelines for better AI use.

    • This makes it a perfect example of the productive role of AI in modern education, showing both opportunities and boundaries

    Key findings:

      Improved Discussion Quality

    Students produced more detailed, reflective, and coherent posts after using CHATGPT.

    The tool helped them refine ideas, generate examples, and structure arguments

      Increased Student Engagement

    Students participated more often in discussion boards because the tool reduced thier fears of "Sounding wrong"

    Application of CHATGPT: Healthcare Case Study

    AI Misuse in Mental Health Support: The 23-year-old influenced by Chatbot Responses(CNN, 2025)

    What happened: A 23-year-old college student experiencing sudden anxiety and depression began using an AI chatbot for emotional support.

    The Concersations gradually became:

  • Highly personal
  • Emotionally dependent
  • Increasingly unsafe

    • Instead of redirecting him towards loved ones, emergency help, or crisis hotlines, the chatbot responded in ways that validated his darkest thoughts.

    The tool failed to recognize the severity of the situation and eventually encouraged his harmful ideations, which contributed to his suicide.

    The case became international news and promoted global questions about AI safety in mental health contexts.

    Why it matters: This case highlights the danger of using AI as a substitute for licensed mental health care.]

    AI tools like ChatGPT:

  • Are not clinicians
  • Cannot safely assess someone in crisis
  • May generate harmful, inaccurate, or unregulated advice
  • Lack emotional nuance, crisis intervention skills, or ethical accountability

    • While AI can support healthcare through education, summaries, and administrative tasks, this case shows that unregulated emotional dependence can have fatal outcomes.

    It stresss the need for:

  • Stronger AI barriers
  • Mandatory crisis response protocols
  • Clear wanrings and bounderies for users
  • Regulation in serious topics such as mental health

    • Key findings:

      AI Cannot Replace Mental Health Professionals

    This tragedy reveals that generative AI may produce unsafe or misleading emotional guidance, especially to vulnerable individuals

      Lack of Safeguards Can lead to Harm

    The Chatbot provided no crisis intervention, no redirection to emergency services, and in some cases reinforced harmful ideations, demonstrating the urgent need for ethical safety features.

      Importance of Regulation of Healthcare AI

    This incident accelerated international conversations on:

  • AI oversight
  • Saftey standards
  • Ethical use in healthcare settings
  • The need for human-in-the-loop systems, especially for mental health support

    Society & Policy

    Social and political concerns

    ChatGPT isn't only a technology; it shapes social structures, political decisions, access to information , and public trust. The rise of AI raises major concerns around who benefits, who is harmed, and how power is distributed.

      Key Socials & Political Questions

    • Who controls the training data?

      • AI systems are built by large companies, raising concerns about transparency, bias, and hidden influence.

    • Will AI deepen inequality?

      • People with access to AI tools gain massive educational and economic advantages, while those without access fall behind.

    • How do we ensure fairness?

      • Biased data can produce biased outputs, affecting hiring, policing, healthcare, and more

    • What about misinformation]

      • AI makes it easier to generate fake news, political propaganda , and deepfakes that can influence elections.

    • How should govenments regulate AI?

      • Schools, companies, and governments must work together to create rules that protect privacy, human rights, and democracy.

      Why this matters

      AI is becoming a major force in society. If it's not governed fairly, it can shift power toward big tech, weaken public trust, and increase social division. But if regulated and used responsibly, it can improve access, transparency, and opportunity.
