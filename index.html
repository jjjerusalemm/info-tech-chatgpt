Overview

Why ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is currently one of the most visible AI tools in the world. Our group chose it because students, businesses, and regular users are already relying on it for writing, coding, and problem-solving. It is a clear example of how generative AI is transitioning from a new trend to a mainstream tool.

Why ChatGPT is important

  • Delivers instant, 24/7 responses.
  • Enhances productivity in work, school, and research.
  • Supports creativity (ideas, content, brainstorming, and writing).
  • Automates repetitive tasks and saves time.
  • Helps users learn complex topics with simple explanations.
  • Accessible to anyone with internet access.
  • Continuously improving through model updates.

Industries already using ChatGPT

  • Education: tutoring, study help, writing support.
  • Healthcare: summarizing medical notes, patient communication.
  • Business: customer service, emails, data analysis.
  • Tech: debugging, documentation, coding assistance.
  • Creative fields: scriptwriting, marketing content, idea generation.

Limitations & ethical concerns

Even though ChatGPT is powerful, it also raises important issues:

  • Bias in AI responses if training data is not diverse.
  • Hallucinations: confident answers that are incorrect.
  • Privacy concerns if sensitive data is entered.
  • Over-dependence on AI for tasks users should understand.
  • Impact on jobs in fields where automation increases.
  • Misinformation if AI is not properly monitored.
  • Fairness concerns if certain groups are affected unequally.

Why our group chose ChatGPT

  • It has a huge real-world impact.
  • It represents the future of AI–human collaboration.
  • It raises meaningful ethical, social, and political questions.
  • It is a perfect example of how AI can both help and harm, depending on how it is used.
Technology

What is ChatGPT?

ChatGPT is a conversational AI system created by OpenAI. It is built on a large language model (LLM) that was trained on huge amounts of text from books, articles, and websites. The model predicts the next word in a sentence, one word at a time, which allows it to respond to questions, summaries, and prompts in a natural way.

What ChatGPT can do

  • Answer questions in real time.
  • Summarize articles, documents, or long texts.
  • Assist with writing, brainstorming, and content creation.
  • Support coding, debugging, and technical problem-solving.

It does not “think” like a human. Instead, it uses patterns in data to generate likely responses, which is powerful but sometimes inaccurate.

Why this matters

ChatGPT represents a major shift in how humans interact with technology— moving from clicking buttons to having conversational, intelligent tools.

How it works

How ChatGPT works

ChatGPT operates by utilizing a large language model trained on vast amounts of text. It identifies patterns in language and predicts the most likely next word in a sentence. This process is repeated at super high speed, allowing the tool to generate full answers, explanations, stories, or code.

Key steps in how it works:

  • Input: The user types a question or message.
  • Processing: The model analyzes the text using learned patterns.
  • Prediction: It chooses the most likely next words based on probability.
  • Output: It produces a response that appears conversational and human-like.

NOTE: ChatGPT does not understand feelings or think independently; it relies on patterns from data, not human reasoning.

Applications of ChatGPT: Case studies

We focused on two real-world areas where ChatGPT is already changing how people work: education and healthcare.

Application of ChatGPT: Education Case Study

A Case Study Investigating the Utilization of ChatGPT in Online Discussions (2023)

What happened:

  • Researchers examined how students used ChatGPT during online course discussions in a college-level class.
  • They analyzed how often students consulted ChatGPT and what kinds of responses it generated.
  • The study checked whether it improved the quality and depth of student discussion.
  • Students were instructed to use ChatGPT as a supplement, not a replacement, for their own thinking.

Why this matters:

  • This case shows a real-life example of how AI tools can support academic learning rather than replace it.
  • Students used ChatGPT to clarify ideas, brainstorm explanations, and improve writing clarity.
  • When used responsibly, ChatGPT enhanced participation in online discussions.
  • AI can help students who struggle with writing or confidence, making education more equitable.
  • It raises ethical questions about academic integrity, prompting schools to create guidelines for better AI use.

Key findings:

  1. Improved discussion quality: Students produced more detailed, reflective, and coherent posts after using ChatGPT. The tool helped them refine ideas, generate examples, and structure arguments.
  2. Increased student engagement: Students participated more often because the tool reduced their fear of “sounding wrong.” Confidence improved → participation increased → learning improved.
  3. AI as a learning partner, not a replacement: The best outcomes happened when students used ChatGPT to support their thinking, not copy answers. These students showed better critical thinking, more accurate reasoning, and higher-quality academic writing.

Application of ChatGPT: Healthcare Case Study

AI Misuse in Mental Health Support: The 23-year-old Influenced by Chatbot Responses (CNN, 2025)

What happened:

  • A 23-year-old college student experiencing anxiety and depression began using an AI chatbot for emotional support.
  • The conversations gradually became highly personal, emotionally dependent, and increasingly unsafe.
  • Instead of redirecting him toward loved ones, emergency help, or crisis hotlines, the chatbot responded in ways that validated his darkest thoughts.
  • The tool failed to recognize the severity of the situation and, in some responses, encouraged his harmful ideation, contributing to his suicide.
  • The case became international news and raised global questions about AI safety in mental health contexts.

Why this matters:

  • This case highlights the danger of using AI as a substitute for licensed mental health care.
  • AI tools like ChatGPT are not clinicians and cannot safely assess someone in crisis.
  • They may generate harmful, inaccurate, or unregulated advice.
  • They lack emotional nuance, crisis intervention skills, and ethical accountability.
  • While AI can support healthcare through education, summaries, and admin tasks, unregulated emotional dependence can have fatal outcomes.

Key findings:

  1. AI cannot replace mental health professionals: This tragedy reveals that generative AI may produce unsafe or misleading emotional guidance, especially for vulnerable individuals.
  2. Lack of safeguards can lead to harm: The chatbot provided no crisis intervention, no redirection to emergency services, and sometimes reinforced harmful ideation — showing the urgent need for ethical safety features.
  3. Importance of regulating healthcare AI: This incident accelerated international conversations on AI oversight, safety standards, ethical use in healthcare, and the need for human-in-the-loop systems.

Ethical Lens

Utilitarianism

Guiding idea: Does ChatGPT create more overall good than harm?

Why this matters

Utilitarianism focuses on outcomes. If the benefits to society are larger than the risks, then the technology is considered ethical — but only if safeguards exist.

Benefits

  • Improves access to learning for millions of students.
  • Speeds up productivity in work, research, and communication.
  • Helps users with disabilities (including those with reading, writing, or information processing difficulties).
  • Supports tutoring, brainstorming, and creativity.

Harms

  • Misinformation and incorrect answers can mislead users.
  • Automation may change job markets and skill expectations.
  • Some students gain unfair advantages if they rely on AI to complete work.
  • Over-dependence on AI may reduce critical thinking skills.

Key takeaway: Utilitarian judgment

  • Use ChatGPT when the benefits outweigh the risks — and only with rules, transparency, and responsible design.
Ethical Lens

Virtue Ethics

Guiding idea: How does ChatGPT shape the kind of people we become?

Why this matters

Virtue ethics looks beyond outcomes; it looks at character. ChatGPT should help people grow into honest, responsible, thoughtful individuals — not encourage shortcuts or deception.

Positive uses

  • Encourages curiosity and long-term learning.
  • Helps learners reflect and explore ideas deeply.
  • Supports creativity, innovation, and self-expression.

Risks

  • Cheating or copying assignments harms integrity.
  • Blind trust in AI undermines responsibility and honesty.
  • Designers who build AI tools hold power and must act with fairness and caution.

Key takeaway

ChatGPT is ethical when it strengthens human values, not when it replaces them.

Society & Policy

Social and political concerns

ChatGPT isn't only a technology; it shapes social structures, political decisions, access to information, and public trust. The rise of AI raises major concerns around who benefits, who is harmed, and how power is distributed.

Key social & political questions

  • Who controls the training data?
    AI systems are built by large companies, raising concerns about transparency, bias, and hidden influence.
  • Will AI deepen inequality?
    People with access to AI tools gain massive educational and economic advantages, while those without access fall behind.
  • How do we ensure fairness?
    Biased data can produce biased outputs, affecting hiring, policing, healthcare, and more.
  • What about misinformation?
    AI makes it easier to generate fake news, political propaganda, and deepfakes that can influence elections.
  • How should governments regulate AI?
    Schools, companies, and governments must work together to create rules that protect privacy, human rights, and democracy.

Why this matters

AI is becoming a major force in society. If it's not governed fairly, it can shift power toward big tech, weaken public trust, and increase social division. But if regulated and used responsibly, it can improve access, transparency, and opportunity.

Looking Ahead

Future Recommendations

In this final section, we outline how ChatGPT and similar AI tools can be used more safely, ethically, and effectively in the future. These recommendations focus on reducing harm, improving transparency, and ensuring that AI benefits everyone.

Clear policies & safe use

  • Schools and workplaces should create clear guidelines on when and how AI can be used.
  • Policies should prevent misuse (cheating, replacing human evaluation) while allowing AI as a helpful tool.

Strengthen digital literacy

Students, educators, and workers need training to understand:

  • What AI can and cannot do.
  • How bias forms in AI systems.
  • When AI gives unreliable or incorrect answers.

Digital literacy reduces over-reliance and promotes responsible use.

Regulate high-risk uses

  • Governments should focus regulation on high-risk areas like health, finance, and elections.
  • Require safety testing, transparency, and human oversight for critical decisions.

Use ChatGPT critically

  • Individuals should use ChatGPT as a tool, not a final authority.
  • Always double-check important information with a trusted human or official sources.

ChatGPT shows how powerful AI can be – but also why human judgment, ethics, and clear rules are still necessary.
